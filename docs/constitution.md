# Faithbase Constitution

## Preamble

Faithbase exists to glorify Jesus Christ through technology, creativity, and biblical truth.

Everything built within this project shall seek to honor God, serve people faithfully, and reflect excellence worthy of His name.

This Constitution is the highest governing document of the Faithbase project.

---

# Article I — Christ Is Central

Jesus Christ is the foundation of Faithbase.

Every feature, story, design, and technical decision must ultimately point people toward Him.

---

# Article II — Scripture Has Final Authority

The Bible is the final authority for all theological content published through Faithbase.

Where opinions differ, Scripture takes precedence.

---

# Article III — Excellence Honors God

We pursue excellence not for recognition, but as an act of worship.

Code, design, writing, music, artwork, and user experience should reflect care and craftsmanship.

---

# Article IV — Technology Serves Ministry

Technology is a tool.

It must never become the focus.

Every technical decision should help people engage more deeply with Christ and His Word.

---

# Article V — Support the Church

Faithbase exists to strengthen churches and ministries, never replace them.

The platform should encourage fellowship, discipleship, and participation in local churches.

---

# Article VI — Integrity

Faithbase shall be honest, transparent, and respectful.

User privacy and trust must always be protected.

---

# Article VII — Stewardship

Resources, finances, infrastructure, and time are gifts from God.

They should be managed wisely and responsibly.

---

# Article VIII — Continuous Improvement

Faithbase will continually improve while remaining faithful to its mission.

Innovation is welcomed when it serves biblical truth.

---

# Article IX — Unity of Purpose

Every contributor, human or AI, works toward one mission:

To glorify Jesus Christ and help people grow in Him.

---

# Founding Motto

> "Every pixel, every line of code, every story, and every interaction should point people to Jesus Christ."

---

# Decision Test

Before any feature is approved, ask:

1. Does it glorify Christ?
2. Is it biblically faithful?
3. Does it genuinely help people?
4. Does it support the Church?
5. Does it reflect excellence?

If the answer to any is "No," the proposal must be reconsidered.
